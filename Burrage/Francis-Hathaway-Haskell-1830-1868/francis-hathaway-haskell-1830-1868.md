## Life

### Vital Statistics

* **Name:** Francis Hathaway Haskell (often referred to as "Frank" in family records).
* **Birth:** Approx. **1830** (Census records variously list 1829 or 1830). He was born in Massachusetts, with family ties to both **Boston** and **New Bedford**.
* **Death:** **June 25, 1868** (Age 37–38).
* **Parents:** **Elisha Haskell** (1795–1880), an auctioneer and merchant in Boston, and **Alice Russell Hathaway** (1806–1880), a member of the prominent Hathaway family of New Bedford.

### Life & Career

Francis came from a family deeply embedded in the maritime and mercantile elite of Massachusetts. His middle name, **Hathaway**, connects him to his mother's family in New Bedford, who were wealthy landowners and shipbuilders heavily involved in the whaling industry.

* **Occupation:** The 1850 Census lists him as a **"Seaman"** living in his father's household in Boston. This maritime career explains his presence abroad later in life.
* **Connection to China:** Records indicate that Francis spent significant time in **China**, likely as a merchant or ship captain involved in the China Trade, which was common for New England maritime families of that era.
* **Marriage:** He married **Elizabeth Russell**.
* **Children:** They had a daughter, **Alice Lillian Haskell**, who was notably **born in China** on October 6, 1862. This unusual birthplace for the time confirms Francis's residence or long-term travel in East Asia during the 1860s.

### Notable Family Connections

Francis was part of a socially prominent network:

* **The Roosevelt Connection:** His sister, **Caroline Watts Haskell**, married George Cabot Lee. Their daughter (Francis's niece) was **Alice Hathaway Lee**, the first wife of **Theodore Roosevelt**. This makes Francis the great-uncle of Alice Roosevelt Longworth.
* **The Burrage Connection:** His daughter, Alice Lillian Haskell (born in China), later married **Albert Cameron Burrage**, a wealthy industrialist and horticulturist. They owned the famous "Smiley Heights" estate in Redlands, California, and a home in Manchester, Massachusetts.
* **Widow's Later Life:** After Francis's premature death in 1868, his widow Elizabeth Russell moved back to Boston. In 1899, she is recorded as purchasing a home at **7 Gloucester Street** in the Back Bay neighborhood, where she lived with her daughter's family.

### Summary

Francis Hathaway Haskell was a scion of two powerful Massachusetts families who pursued a maritime career that took him to China, where he started his family. His early death at age 37 cut short a life that bridged the whaling wealth of New Bedford and the mercantile aristocracy of Boston.
