# David Fulton (1771–1843)

## Life

David Fulton (January 2, 1771 – August 7, 1843) was an Irish-American pioneer, civic leader, and the patriarch of one of Arkansas’s most prominent political families.

He is best known as the **4th Mayor of Little Rock** and for his role as the **Surveyor General of Public Lands** for Arkansas. He was also the father of William Savin Fulton, the last territorial governor of Arkansas and one of its first U.S. Senators.

### Early Life and Migration
* **Origins:** Born in County Donegal, Ireland.
* **Immigration:** He emigrated to the United States, settling first in Maryland. There, he married **Elizabeth Savin** in 1795. The couple later lived in Alabama before moving west.
* **Move to Arkansas:** Fulton moved to Little Rock in 1833 to be closer to his son, William Savin Fulton, who had been appointed Secretary of the Arkansas Territory by President Andrew Jackson.

### Career and Public Service
Upon arriving in Little Rock, Fulton became a central figure in the developing capital's business and political life.

* **Mayor of Little Rock (1835):** Fulton served as the fourth mayor of Little Rock. His term is historically notable because it was cut short when Little Rock transitioned from a "town" to a "city" government structure later that year, necessitating new elections.
* **Surveyor General:** In 1838, President Martin Van Buren appointed him Surveyor General of Public Lands for Arkansas. This was a critical role in a frontier state where land claims and boundaries were matters of intense legal and economic importance.
* **Business:** He owned and operated a local tanning business known as the "Tan Yard."
* **Civic Roles:** He served as a Justice of the Peace, a Pulaski County Judge (1836–1838), and was the president of the local Anti-Gambling Society.

### Family and Legacy
Fulton was the head of a powerful political dynasty in early Arkansas history.
* **Wife:** Elizabeth Savin Fulton (died 1829 in Alabama).
* **Notable Children:**
  * **William Savin Fulton (1795–1844):** Served as the Governor of Arkansas Territory (1835–1836) and U.S. Senator (1836–1844). At one point, David Fulton was serving as Mayor of Little Rock while his son was serving as the Territorial Governor.
  * **Jane Juliet Shall:** Moved her family to Little Rock to join her father and brother; her descendants helped preserve the historic **Hinderliter House**, the oldest building in Little Rock.

### Death
David Fulton died on August 7, 1843, at the age of 72. He is buried in **Mount Holly Cemetery** in Little Rock, a historic site often called the "Westminster Abbey of Arkansas" due to the number of notable figures interred there.
