Below is a list of sources used to compile the information in the genealogy report and name history for the Armour family. These sources were referenced either directly or indirectly through the provided data, web searches, and genealogical databases. Some information, particularly for earlier generations (pre-1700s), relies on user-submitted family trees, which are noted as less reliable unless corroborated by primary records.

Sources for Armour Family Genealogy Report and Name History

Geni.com:

Used for detailed profiles of later Armour family members, including Norman Armour Sr (1887–1982), Norman Armour Jr (1920–1978), and Norman Theo Armour III (b. 1947). Geni provided family connections, birth/death dates, and biographical details, especially for the American branch.
Example: Norman Armour Sr’s profile includes diplomatic career and marriage to Princess Maria Koudachev.

WikiTree:

Provided genealogical data for Scottish ancestors, including Alexander Armour (1655–1740), Robert Armour (1692–1798), and George Armour Sr (1812–1881). WikiTree entries helped confirm locations like Campbeltown and Kilmarnock.
Example: Robert Armour’s profile links to Trodigal Farm and whisky still production.

FamilySearch.org:

Contributed birth, death, and marriage records for Scottish and American Armours, particularly Alexander Armour (b. 1655, Fenwick), Archibald Armour Sr (1782–1828), and George Armour Sr (1812–1881). Also verified parents like Archibald and Margaret Turner.
Example: FSID for George Armour confirms Campbeltown birth and Chicago residence.

Ancestry.com:

User-submitted family trees provided data for early generations (e.g., Dauid/David Armour, 1546–1625; Hew Armour, b. ~1590; John/James Armour, 1631–1684). These are less reliable due to lack of primary sources but align with provided query details.
Example: Trees link David Armour to Canongate, Edinburgh.

Wikipedia:

Detailed George Armour Sr’s (1812–1881) biography as a Chicago grain merchant and his distinction from brother Philip Danforth Armour (Armour & Company). Also provided context for Norman Armour Sr’s diplomatic career.
Example: George Armour Wikipedia page.

Find A Grave Index:

Verified George Allison Armour’s (1856–1936) birth in Chicago and death in Princeton, NJ, with burial details.
Example: Memorial ID for George Allison Armour.

The Kintyre Magazine:

Referenced for Robert Armour (b. 1692) and family ties to Trodigal Farm, Campbeltown, and whisky still production (copper smithing).
Example: Article on Campbeltown’s economic history.

Forebears.io:

Provided surname distribution data for Armour, noting prevalence in Ayrshire (1,171 bearers in 1881 UK Census) and global spread (7,376 bearers in 2014, including 3,409 in the US).
Example: Forebears Armour Surname.

Surname Database (surnamedb.com):

Explained the occupational origin of Armour from Old French armure and its spelling variants (Armorer, Armer). Noted early Scottish records and phonetic variations.
Example: Surname Database: Armour.

Dictionary of American Family Names (Oxford University Press, 2003):

Cited via House of Names for the definition of Armour as a Scottish/northern Irish occupational surname tied to armor-making.
Example: Referenced in name history section.

House of Names:

Provided historical context for Armour surname in Ayrshire and its association with Clan Campbell territories.
Example: House of Names: Armour.

British Surnames:

Supplied 1881 UK Census data showing 74.9% of Armour bearers in Scotland, with Ayrshire as a primary hub.
Example: British Surnames: Armour.

RootsWeb:

Contributed genealogical data for early Armours, including William (b. ~1605) and Alexander (b. 1655), with ties to Fenwick and Kilmarnock.
Example: RootsWeb mailing list archives for Ayrshire families.
