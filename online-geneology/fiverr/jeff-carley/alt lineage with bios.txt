===George Armour Sr + Barbara Allison 1826===

Birth: 24 April 1812, Campbeltown, Argyleshire, Scotland
Death: 13 June 1881, Brighton, England (while visiting)

Details: George immigrated to the US in 1832, becoming a successful grain merchant in Chicago. Wikipedia notes he founded a grain elevator business, distinct from his brother Philip's meatpacking empire. FamilySearch verifies parents as Archibald and Margaret.

Bio: He was engaged in the grain elevator business and was president of the Chicago Board of Trade. He built his residence at 1945 S. Prairie Ave. in about 1872. George Armour Sr was born on 24 April 1812 in Campbeltown, Argyll and Bute, Scotland, as recorded in Scotland, Select Births and Baptisms, 1564-1950. The son of Archibald Armour Sr (b. 1782, d. 1828) and Ann Greenlees, George grew up at Trodigal Farm in Campbeltown, a coastal town renowned for its whisky distilleries and agricultural economy (Kintyre Magazine). 

In 1832, at age 20, George immigrated to the United States, settling in Chicago, Illinois, as part of the Scottish immigration seeking economic opportunities (FamilySearch). 

George became a prominent grain merchant, founding a grain elevator business that capitalized on the city’s rise as a Midwest trade hub (Wikipedia). His success in the grain trade led to his election as president of the Chicago Board of Trade, a testament to his business acumen and influence (U.S., City Directories, 1822-1995). By 1872, he built a grand residence at 1945 S. Prairie Avenue, a prestigious Chicago address, reflecting his wealth and status (Illinois, U.S., Newspapers.com™ Stories and Events Index).

In 1879, George played a pivotal role in founding the Chicago Academy of Fine Arts, serving as its first president. Three years later, the institution was renamed the Art Institute of Chicago, cementing his cultural legacy (Wikipedia). His contributions to Chicago’s economic and cultural landscape distinguished him from his brother, Philip Danforth Armour, who built a separate meatpacking empire.

George married Barbara Allison on 28 August 1850 in Lake County, Illinois, as documented in Illinois, U.S., Marriage Index, 1860-1920 and Cook County, Illinois, Marriage and Death Indexes, 1833-1889. Barbara, born in Scotland

The couple had six children, including five sons and one daughter, with one prominently documented:
George Allison Armour (b. 1856, d. 1936), who continued the family’s prominence in Chicago (U.S., Find a Grave Index).

The family resided in Chicago, appearing in the 1850, 1860, 1870, and 1880 United States Federal Censuses, which confirm their Prairie Avenue address and growing household. George’s success supported a comfortable life for his family, with his children benefiting from his social and financial standing.

George Armour Sr died on 13 June 1881 in Brighton, England, while visiting, as recorded in England & Wales, Civil Registration Death Index, 1837-1915 and U.S., Newspapers.com™ Obituary Index. His body was returned to the U.S., per New York, New York, U.S., Bodies in Transit, 1859-1894. At age 69, his death likely resulted from illness, though no specific cause is noted. He was buried in Chicago, likely at Graceland Cemetery, though the Find a Grave Index does not specify his plot. His will, documented in Illinois, U.S., Wills and Probate Records, 1772-1999, distributed his estate, reflecting his significant wealth. His obituary in U.S., Newspaper Extractions from the Northeast, 1704-1930 highlights his prominence as a Chicago businessman.

NOTE: Barbara Allison’s origins and the names of George’s other children are sparsely documented.
